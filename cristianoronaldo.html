Ronaldo: His Rise to Fame

Early Life

Cristiano Ronaldo dos Santos Aveiro, was, and still is, one of the most legendary players that ever existed. Born February 5, 1985,
his father was the equipment manager for the local boy's soccer club Andorinha. Through this, Ronaldo was introduced to the game of soccer.
This was where Ronaldo's love for soccer grew. Although conditions were not stable in their household, Ronaldo's love for soccer
grew so much that by age 10, as his godfather said,"All he wanted to do as a boy was to play football(soccer).
He loved the game so much he'd miss meals or escape out of his bedroom window with a ball when he was supposed to do his homework."

Signed on by Manchester United

In 2001, after a stint with Nacional da liha da Madeira, Ronaldo was signed on by Manchester United. He was the youngest player on the team(just 16), and on top of all that, was paid more than 12 million euros! He did not let the team and the fans down, scoring the first three goals and thus helping his team clinch the 2004 FA Cup final. Wowed by his performance, they signed a five-year contract for 31 million euro contract. He did another stunning performance in the 2008 FIFA World Cup, setting the record for most goals scored(42), and getting FIFA World Player of the Year For 2008. He proved to be a very legendary player for Manchester United.

Switching Teams

In 2009, Real Madrid paid Manchester a whopping $131 million to sign on Ronaldo on their team. No one was surprised, as speculation had arose that Ronaldo was going to switch teams. While on Real Madrid, he earned himself and his team an impressive amount of titles. like the 2014 Champions League Title and winning the 4th Ballon d'Or in December 2016, beating Messi in the number of titles won.

Goodbye, Real Madrid

After nine years on Real, he decided to part with Madrid, joining the Italian Serie A Club Juventus. He even posted a message for Real fans, saying that he could not thank them enough for all the support and love they gave. On Juventus, his debut season was a successful one, and after getting 8 consecutive Serie A titles, he was named the league's MVP in 2019. He still plays on Juventus to this day, but there is speculation that he might switch back to Manchester.
